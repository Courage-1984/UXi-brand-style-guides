06 — Brand

Vision, Mission & Voice

Approved messaging and brand language for ASCON digital properties. Use the Vision and Mission wording below consistently — do not paraphrase or abbreviate on official platforms.

Vision

To be the leading Pan-African professional confederation for supply chain excellence — driving harmonisation, professionalism, and collaboration to position Africa's supply chain as a competitive global leader.

Mission

To unify professional African supply chain standards, strengthen institutions, and build a connected, future-ready ecosystem that enables practitioners and organisations to thrive in global value chains.

Core Values
Professionalism
Integrity, ethics, and accountability in all we do.
Relevance
Equity and inclusivity in practice across all member nations.
Collaborate & Grow
Mutually beneficial partnerships that elevate the whole sector.
Measurable Impact
Sustainability, long-term value, and evidence-based outcomes.
Future Readiness
Excellence, innovation, agility, and transformation at all levels.

07 — Brand

Logo Usage Rules

Use the official lockup consistently. Maintain clear space around the logo and avoid stretching, recolouring, or placing on low-contrast backgrounds.

A
ASCON
African Supply Chain Confederation
Correct — Dark Background
Use on brand navy (#0D2240 / #071628). Gold mark + white wordmark.
A
ASCON
African Supply Chain Confederation
Correct — Light Background
Navy wordmark on light/white surfaces. Gold mark always retained.
A
ASCON
African Supply Chain Confederation
Incorrect — Low Contrast / Distorted
No stretching, recolouring, or busy/low-contrast backgrounds.

✓ Do

  • Use the primary lockup (mark + wordmark) for all digital headers
  • Apply the gold mark consistently — never replace it with another colour
  • Use the Navy wordmark on light surfaces; White on dark
  • Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the 'A' mark
  • Use the monogram mark alone only for very small applications (favicon, stamps)

✗ Don't

  • Stretch, skew, rotate, or distort any part of the lockup
  • Replace the gold mark with another brand colour or greyscale
  • Place the logo on low-contrast or busy photographic backgrounds
  • Use unapproved typefaces as part of or alongside the logo
  • Recreate the logo in PowerPoint, Word, or any vector editor
