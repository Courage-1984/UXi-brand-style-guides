Motion

05 — Motion

ANIMATION

Motion is purposeful and restrained. It communicates state changes, guides attention, and provides feedback. Never use animation purely for decoration.

▶
Micro
150ms · ease-out
Button press, focus ring, active states
◆
Standard
250ms · ease-in-out
Hover, colour transitions, tooltips
●
Entrance
400ms · cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)
Modal open, page transition, card reveal

Reduced motion: Always respect prefers-reduced-motion. Wrap animations in a media query and fall back to instant state changes. This is both an accessibility requirement and good engineering practice.

Buttons

06 — Buttons

BUTTON SYSTEM

Buttons are direct action triggers. Use the Primary button for the single most important action per page. Secondary and Ghost for supporting actions. Never use more than two button variants in the same visual group.

Variants

Apply Now Learn More View Fees Contact Us Withdraw Closed

Sizes

Large — Apply Now Medium — Apply Now Small

On Dark Background

Apply Now Learn More View Fees

Usage rules: Button labels must use action-oriented language (verb + noun). Labels are uppercase, 8–12px letter-spacing for legibility at small sizes. Minimum click target: 44×44px (WCAG 2.5.5). All buttons must have a visible focus ring for keyboard navigation.

07
Form Elements

07 — Form Elements

FORMS

Forms are high-friction moments. Every design decision — label placement, spacing, error feedback — must reduce the cognitive load on applicants and reduce drop-off.

As it appears on your ID document.
⚠ Please enter a valid 13-digit South African ID number.
Submit Application

Label position: Always above the input field (never inside as placeholder). Placeholders are supplementary hints only — never replace labels.

Focus state: All interactive fields must have a visible turquoise focus ring: box-shadow: 0 0 0 3px rgba(42,210,201,0.25). This is a WCAG 2.4.7 requirement.

Error messages: Display inline below the field in Blaze Red (#EB473E). Errors must be conveyed both visually (border colour) and textually (message). Never use colour alone.

Required fields: Mark with a red asterisk (*) adjacent to the label. Provide a legend: "Fields marked * are required" at the top of the form.

Imagery

11 — Imagery

PHOTOGRAPHY

Photography must reflect the diversity, ambition, and real human energy of the Belgium Campus community. Images should feel authentic, not staged — real students, real environments, real work.

HERO IMAGE
Hero / Full-bleed: Wide-angle, high contrast, dramatic lighting. Show students engaged in real lab or project work. Dark overlays (50–70% opacity) must maintain text legibility.
PORTRAIT
Student portraits: Candid and purposeful. Natural light preferred. Diverse representation is non-negotiable — South Africa's demographics must be reflected in all photography.
CAMPUS / TECH
Environment shots: Campuses, labs, code on screens. Keep tech props authentic. Prefer wide-aperture shots for depth. Maintain a cool-toned or neutral colour grade.

✓ Use images that are...

  • Authentic and documentary in feel
  • Diverse and representative
  • High resolution (min 1920px wide)
  • Focused and purposeful in composition
  • Consistent in colour temperature

✗ Avoid images that are...

  • Generic stock photography
  • Predominantly non-African faces
  • Over-saturated or filtered
  • Cluttered or visually noisy
  • Low resolution or pixelated

Voice & Tone

13 — Voice & Tone

VOICE & TONE

Belgium Campus iTversity speaks with clarity, confidence, and warmth. We never talk down to our audience — we invite them to rise with us. Tone shifts with context: authoritative in academic contexts, encouraging with applicants, energetic in campaign materials.

Quality 01
BOLD, NOT ARROGANT
We make direct, confident statements. We don't hedge or qualify with unnecessary caveats. We believe in what we offer and we say so clearly.
Quality 02
INSPIRING, NOT PREACHY
We paint a picture of possibility. We motivate through aspiration, not guilt or fear. We celebrate student achievement loudly and authentically.
Quality 03
CLEAR, NOT JARGON-HEAVY
We explain IT concepts plainly. Our audience includes first-generation students — accessibility in language is as important as accessibility in design.
Quality 04
HUMAN, NOT CORPORATE
We use "you" and "we". We speak to people, not applicants. We avoid passive voice and bureaucratic constructions wherever possible.
✓ Write this ✗ Not this

"Apply for 2026 — secure your place today."

"Prospective students are encouraged to submit applications timeously."

"Study IT. Heal the world."

"Information and Communication Technology qualifications are available."

"Your future in tech starts here."

"The institution provides multiple pathways for entry into the IT sector."

Accessibility

14 — Accessibility

WCAG 2.1 AA

Accessibility is not optional — it is legally required under South Africa's Electronic Communications Act and morally essential for an institution that includes deaf students and students with diverse abilities. Belgium Campus iTversity must meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA as a minimum.

👁️
Perceivable
All text must meet 4.5:1 contrast. Images need descriptive alt text. Captions required for all video content. No information conveyed by colour alone.
⌨️
Operable
All functionality accessible by keyboard. Focus indicators must be visible. No keyboard traps. Minimum touch target 44×44px. Skip-to-content link required.
📖
Understandable
Clear language, consistent navigation, and descriptive error messages. Form labels always visible. Language attribute set on <html> element.
🤝
Robust
Valid, semantic HTML. ARIA roles and attributes used correctly. Compatible with major screen readers (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver). Regular automated and manual audits.
🎯
Focus Management
Turquoise focus ring on all interactive elements: outline: 2px solid #2AD2C9; outline-offset: 2px;. Never suppress outlines with outline: none without a custom replacement.
🦻
Deaf Student Support
As BC has a Diploma for Deaf Students, all video content must include closed captions (auto-generated captions are insufficient). Sign language interpreted videos are recommended.

Recommended tools: Run automated checks with axe DevTools and Lighthouse on every page deploy. Supplement with manual keyboard testing and screen reader testing (VoiceOver on macOS / NVDA on Windows). Target a Lighthouse Accessibility score of ≥ 95.

UX Recommendations

15 — UX Recommendations

IMPROVING THE EXPERIENCE

The following are targeted improvements to reduce cognitive load and improve conversion without altering the brand's visual identity. Each recommendation is scoped and actionable.

01
Simplify the navigation hierarchy
The current multi-level dropdown structure (3+ levels deep) creates significant cognitive overload, especially on mobile. Consolidate into a maximum of two levels with a mega-menu for primary sections. Group related content under "Study at BC" and "Our Programmes" into scannable columns rather than nested lists.
Navigation Accessibility
02
Make "Apply Now" persistently visible
The primary conversion goal of the site is student applications. The "Apply Now" CTA should be present in the sticky navigation at all times — styled as a Turquoise primary button — not buried in a sub-menu dropdown. On mobile, this should appear as a full-width sticky bar at the bottom of the viewport.
Navigation Visual
03
Consolidate the top utility bar
The current site has a pre-header utility bar with links (Student Portal, Alumni, etc.) that duplicates some main navigation items and adds cognitive overhead. Move Student Portal to a persistent icon in the header. Move Alumni and less-critical links to the footer only. This reduces the number of navigation layers from three to one.
Navigation Content
04
Add a programme comparison tool
Prospective students often need to compare qualifications side-by-side. A lightweight "compare" feature on the qualifications pages — letting users select 2–3 programmes and see duration, entry requirements, and career paths in one view — would significantly reduce back-and-forth navigation and aid decision-making.
Content Visual
05
Improve hero slider accessibility and engagement
Auto-playing carousels can disorient users and fail WCAG 2.2.2 (Pause, Stop, Hide). Replace the auto-rotating hero slider with a static hero section featuring a bold headline and a single, contextual CTA. If the carousel is retained, it must have accessible pause controls, keyboard navigation, and respect prefers-reduced-motion.
Accessibility Visual
06
Establish consistent section rhythm
Each major page section should follow a consistent pattern: eyebrow label → heading → lead paragraph → content → CTA. This creates predictable scanning behaviour and reduces the mental effort required to parse each page. Currently, sections vary widely in structure, which increases cognitive load.
Content Visual
07
Optimise for mobile-first loading
Given that the majority of South African internet traffic is mobile, images should be served in WebP format with responsive srcset. Implement lazy loading for images below the fold. Hero images should be compressed to under 200KB. These improvements directly impact bounce rate and accessibility for users on lower-bandwidth connections.
Performance Accessibility
08
Surface social proof prominently
Testimonials and graduate outcomes (employment rate, top employers) are among the most persuasive elements for prospective students. Currently buried under About Us, these should appear on the homepage and qualification pages. Short, authentic video testimonials (30–60 seconds) are especially effective for this audience.

Do's & Don'ts

16 — Do's & Don'ts

BRAND RULES

These rules protect brand consistency and accessibility. They apply to all digital and print executions produced by internal teams or external partners.

✓ Do — Typography

Use Bebas Neue exclusively for H1–H3 headings and display text.

Use Avant Garde for all body copy, labels, navigation, and UI text.

Set letter-spacing to 0.02em on Bebas Neue headings for optical comfort.

Use sentence case for all Avant Garde text. Uppercase only for labels and overlines.

✗ Don't — Typography

Never use Bebas Neue for body copy or paragraph text — it is illegible at small sizes.

Never use Arial, Helvetica, or system fonts as a replacement for Avant Garde.

Never mix more than two typefaces in a single layout.

Never set Bebas Neue below 20px — it loses definition and becomes unreadable.

✓ Do — Colour

Use Turquoise (#2AD2C9) on Black or Near Black backgrounds for high-contrast, legible text.

Use Deep Yellow (#FFCB2F) on Black as a highlight and accent colour.

Use White (#FFFFFF) as the primary page background and as reversed text on dark surfaces.

Always verify contrast ratios before applying a new colour combination.

✗ Don't — Colour

Never use Turquoise (#2AD2C9) as body text on white — it fails WCAG AA (1.9:1).

Never use Deep Yellow (#FFCB2F) as text on white — it fails WCAG AA (1.5:1).

Never use Blaze Red as small body text on white backgrounds — fails WCAG AA.

Never introduce new brand colours without reviewing their contrast with all existing palette members.

✓ Do — Layout & Spacing

Maintain generous white space — sections should breathe at 80–96px vertical padding.

Align all content to the 12-column grid with 24px gutters.

Keep content width to a max of 1200px, centred on large screens.

Use consistent 8px-base spacing units throughout all components.

✗ Don't — Layout & Spacing

Never stack hero sections back-to-back — always break with a content section in between.

Never use arbitrary spacing values (e.g. 13px, 22px) — stick to the 8px scale.

Never place more than three items in a single row on mobile viewports.

Never centre-align body paragraphs longer than 2 lines — left-alignment aids readability.

✓ Do — Buttons & CTAs

Use Turquoise Primary buttons for the main action on each screen.

Ensure every button has a clearly visible focus ring for keyboard navigation.

Write button labels in action-verb format: "Apply Now", "View Fees", "Download Brochure".

✗ Don't — Buttons & CTAs

Never use more than two button variants in the same visual group (e.g. Primary + Ghost only).

Never label a button with vague text like "Click Here" or "Submit" without context.

Never remove focus outlines — this makes the site unusable for keyboard-only users.
