06 — Brand

Vision, Mission & Voice

Aligned to the IMM brand architecture and CI manual (2018 + updates): the vision and mission wording below is approved copy that can be used on the website. This section is focused on messaging only; the UI shown here is not an implementation guide for layout or styling.

Vision

To be the private higher education provider of choice and the centre of research and academic excellence for marketing, supply chain, and business management.

Mission

Our mission is to impart knowledge, expertise, and practical skills to produce graduates ready to lead, innovate, and make a global impact.

We deliver this through:

  • Student-focused academic support
  • Industry-aligned curricula
  • Practical and applied learning
  • Credible and globally recognised qualifications
  • Innovation and professional readiness

Purpose

To equip students with the knowledge, skills, and mindset to succeed in their careers and make a lasting global impact.

Core Values (Internal)

Expert Mastery · Thoughtful · Proper · Bold · Collaborative

Core Values (External)

Student-Focused · Industry-Aligned · Credible · Innovative · Connected

Primary Audience

School leavers and first-time students (17-22), plus working professionals and previous graduates (25-45).

Postgraduate students, companies investing in employee upskilling, and African/international students needing flexible delivery.

07 — Brand

Logo Usage Rules

Use the official landscape logo consistently. Maintain clear space around the logo and avoid stretching, skewing, or placing it on low-contrast backgrounds. The grids below are visual guides for alignment and spacing.

Correct — Dark Background
Aligned + Clear space
IMM Graduate School
Use on brand dark surfaces (#0B1E33 / #06111E). Keep consistent sizing and padding.
Correct — Light Background
Clear space guide
IMM Graduate School
Use an approved dark/black logo variant on light backgrounds (avoid low-contrast grey).
Incorrect — Distorted / Low Contrast
Don’t
IMM Graduate School
No stretching, skewing, recolouring, or placing over busy/low-contrast areas.
✓ Do
  • →Use the primary landscape logo for website headers and wide digital layouts
  • →Use the secondary icon/monogram for small applications (icons, stamps, thumbnails)
  • →Apply logo variations for both dark (#0B1E33) and light (#F5F8FC) backgrounds
  • →Use black/white logo variants where full color is not suitable
  • →Keep usage consistent across website header, letterheads and banners
✗ Don't
  • →Use the secondary icon as the default header logo on landscape layouts
  • →Apply only one-color treatment when the full brand palette is required and legible
  • →Place low-contrast logo versions on mismatched backgrounds
  • →Deviate from approved palette values (#FFE500, #F26122, #6DC4E9, #97C93D)
  • →Use unapproved typefaces when Roboto Condensed or approved web fallback is available
08 — Brand

Iconography

Social icons below are current UI references. Photography and imagery direction is included to match the intake: authentic student, lecturer, and campus visuals with balanced aspiration and professionalism.

Imagery & Photography Guidelines
  • Prioritise authentic imagery of real students, lecturers, and campus environments.
  • Use stock or AI visuals minimally and responsibly; avoid generic corporate stereotypes.
  • Reflect both aspirational energy (school leavers) and confident progression (working professionals).
  • Ensure inclusive representation across age, background, and study mode.
  • Keep imagery contextual and realistic to IMM learning and career outcomes.
Social Icons — Current Set

Social icons use a consistent monochrome treatment for clarity and brand cohesion. Keep sizes consistent and place them on dark surfaces for best contrast.

Lines, Margins & Spacing

These layout rules keep pages consistent across the WordPress build. Use the line treatments, content widths, and spacing scales below whenever creating a new page or template.

Line Elements
Page Title Example
Campaign Highlight

Underlines sit directly under titles and are set to roughly 80% of the heading text width. We use both the brand orange underline and a rainbow gradient underline treatment where appropriate.

Line Settings
Colour: var(--imm-color-tech) / #F26122
Height: 3px
Width: ≈80% of H1 text width
Styles: solid brand orange or rainbow gradient
Gradient direction: 90deg
Gradient values: linear-gradient(90deg, #FFE500, #F26122, #6DC4E9, #97C93D)
Alignment: centred under the H1
Spacing above: 8px below H1
Spacing below: 24px before body copy
Margins & Spacing
Content Width ≈ 80% viewport
  • Page content should sit in a centred column of roughly 80% of the viewport width.
  • Keep a minimum of 24–32px vertical space between stacked components.
  • Use larger spacing (40–64px) between major sections like hero → body → footer.
WordPress / Elementor Guidelines
Page container width: 80% viewport (max ~1200px)
Section padding (desktop): 80px top, 80px bottom
Section padding (tablet): 56px top, 56px bottom
Section padding (mobile): 40px top, 40px bottom
Column gap (content blocks): 24–32px
Row gap (cards / grids): 16–24px
